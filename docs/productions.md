Productions 

 

Productions are the system by which players create new items. Every item made during the game — whether a quick meal, a tool, a weapon, a medical remedy, or a complex upgrade — is produced through this mechanic. A production may be simple or demanding: it might require only one player, a single stage, and a few TP, or it might span several stages, involve specialized TP from multiple roles, and leave unfinished items lying around for others to complete. 

 

Items that exist only as starting equipment (such as keys, flour, or gunpowder) or that can only be found during expeditions (such as meat, relics, or scrolls) are not productions. 

 

Initiation: Any player holding the required items in the correct room may start a production. When initiated, an unfinished version of the produced item is created in the room’s inventory. The items used to start it are locked inside this unfinished item. 

Unfinished Items: Unfinished items behave like normal items: they can be picked up, carried away, and left in other rooms. They visibly store both the locked items and the record of accumulated TP. It is common to see many unfinished productions lying around, especially in the Workshop. 

Stages: Each production is divided into one or several stages, each with its own TP quota and room requirement. 

Any number of players may contribute TP to a stage. 

Contributions are permanent: TP spent on a production remains bound to it until the end of the game. There is no decay on TPs spent on productions. 

When one stage is complete, the next becomes immediately available. 

Specialized TP: TP requirements are either general or specialized. If specialized TPs are provided by non-specialists, their cost is tripled (doubled for the Quartermaster). 

Cancellation: Any player may cancel any production in their current room. This destroys the unfinished item and immediately releases the locked items back into the room’s inventory. However, all TP invested in the production is permanently lost. 

Completion: When the final stage is satisfied, the production resolves instantly. The locked items are consumed, and the finished item is automatically picked up by the player who provided the last TP.
